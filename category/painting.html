Paint a Room Like a Pro

● Easy ⏱️ 4-6 hours 💰 $50-150
▼

Professional painters charge $300-500 per room. DIY painting costs $50-150 and gives you full color control. The secret is prep — 80% of a great paint job happens before you open the can.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🎗️ Painter's Tape 🛡️ Drop Cloths 🖌️ Angled Brush 🌀 Paint Roller Set 🦾 Extension Pole
🛒 Shop Painting Supplies
⚠️ Safety First: Open windows and use fans for ventilation — paint fumes can cause headaches and dizziness. Low-VOC and zero-VOC paints are safer for indoor use.
🛋️
Step 1
Move furniture to center of room and cover with drop cloths. Tape plastic over anything too heavy to move.
🎗️
Step 2
Apply painter's tape to trim, ceiling edges, and outlets. FrogTape (green) gives cleanest lines — press firmly with plastic card.
🧱
Step 3
Fill nail holes and cracks with spackle — smooth with putty knife. Let dry 30 min, sand lightly with 220 grit.
🧽
Step 4
Wash walls with TSP or mild detergent — paint won't stick to grease/dust. Kitchens and bathrooms especially need cleaning.
🖌️
Step 5
Cut in edges with angled brush — 2-3 inch band around ceiling, trim, corners. This is where roller can't reach. Take your time.
🌀
Step 6
Roll paint in W pattern, filling in gaps without lifting roller. Work in 3x3 foot sections — 'keep a wet edge' to avoid lap marks.
🎨
Step 7
Wait 4 hours, then apply second coat for true color depth. One coat looks patchy. Two coats looks professional.
✂️
Step 8
Remove tape at 45° angle while paint is slightly tacky (not fully dry). Wait 1-2 hours after final coat. Pulling too late tears paint.

Tip: Buy paint with primer built in (paint + primer) for new drywall or drastic color changes. For same-color refresh, regular paint is fine and cheaper.

Stain a Deck or Fence

● Easy ⏱️ 1 day 💰 $80-200
▼

Staining protects wood from UV damage, moisture, and rot while enhancing the natural grain. A well-maintained stain job lasts 2-3 years and prevents costly deck replacement.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🪵 Deck Stain 🧴 Deck Cleaner 🧽 Stain Pad Applicator 🖌️ Brushes (3 inch)
🛒 Shop Deck Stain
⚠️ Safety First: Stain is highly flammable — no open flames, no smoking. Work when temperature is 50-90°F and no rain for 24-48 hours. Wet stain will wash off or dry unevenly.
🔍
Step 1
Inspect for loose boards, protruding nails, and rot — repair first. Stain won't fix structural problems.
🧴
Step 2
Clean with deck cleaner — scrub with stiff brush, let sit 10-15 min. Brightener restores wood's pH after cleaning, making stain absorb better.
💦
Step 3
Rinse with pressure washer (low setting) or garden hose. Let dry 48 hours — stain on damp wood fails prematurely.
🎨
Step 4
Stain railings and spindles first with brush — work top to bottom. Wipe drips immediately with rag — stain is harder to remove than paint.
🪵
Step 5
Apply stain to deck boards with pad applicator — 3-4 boards at a time. Work with the grain, full length of boards — don't stop mid-board.
🖌️
Step 6
Back-brush with wide brush to work stain into wood and even coverage. This is the pro secret — pad applies, brush finishes.
☀️
Step 7
Let dry 24 hours before light foot traffic, 48 hours for furniture. Oil-based stains take longer — check can for cure times.

Tip: For weathered gray wood, use a brightener after cleaning — it restores the natural wood color before staining. Without brightener, stain looks muddy on gray wood.

Fix Peeling Paint

● Easy ⏱️ 2-3 hours 💰 $20-40
▼

Paint peels when moisture gets behind it or when it was applied to dirty/damp surfaces. Fix the cause first, then the cosmetics — or it'll peel again within months.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🔪 Paint Scraper 📄 Sandpaper (120-220 grit) 🧱 Spackle 🎨 Primer
🛒 Shop Stain-Blocking Primer
⚠️ Safety First: Lead paint in homes built before 1978: don't sand or scrape without testing. Use a lead test kit ($10 at hardware store) — lead dust is toxic.
🔪
Step 1
Scrape away all loose, flaking paint until you hit solid surface. If it moves when you poke it, it needs to come off.
📄
Step 2
Sand the edge where old paint meets bare surface — feather the transition. 220 grit for feathering. Goal: smooth, invisible transition.
🔍
Step 3
Identify why it peeled: moisture? dirty surface? wrong paint type? Bathroom/kitchen = moisture. Exterior = sun/water.
🔧
Step 4
Fix moisture source: exhaust fan, caulk gaps, improve drainage. Without fixing the cause, new paint will peel too.
🧱
Step 5
Patch any exposed substrate with spackle or wood filler. Let dry completely, sand smooth with 120 grit.
🎨
Step 6
Prime bare spots with stain-blocking primer (oil-based for water stains). Primer seals surface and prevents old stains from bleeding through.
🎨
Step 7
Paint with matching color — feather edges into surrounding paint. Two thin coats better than one thick coat.

Tip: For water stains on ceilings, use an oil-based stain-blocking primer like Kilz Original. Water-based primers let the brown stain bleed through — you'll see a shadow after painting.

Paint Kitchen Cabinets

● Medium ⏱️ 2-3 days 💰 $100-200
▼

New cabinets cost $5,000-15,000. Painting them costs $100-200 and takes a weekend. The result looks like a full kitchen remodel if you do the prep right.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🧴 Deglosser 🎨 Cabinet Paint 🌀 Foam Roller 🔩 Screwdriver
🛒 Shop Cabinet Paint
⚠️ Safety First: Work in well-ventilated area — cabinet paint fumes are strong. Remove doors and drawers to paint flat for smoothest finish.
🔩
Step 1
Remove all doors, drawers, and hardware — label everything with tape. Take photos of hinge positions before removing.
🧽
Step 2
Clean thoroughly with TSP or degreaser — kitchen cabinets are greasy. Paint won't stick to grease. Wipe until water sheets, not beads.
📄
Step 3
Sand lightly with 120 grit OR use liquid deglosser on glossy finishes. Deglosser is faster and less messy than sanding.
🎨
Step 4
Apply bonding primer — essential for paint adhesion on slick surfaces. Use shellac-based primer like BIN for oak (blocks wood tannin).
📄
Step 5
Sand dried primer lightly with 220 grit for glass-smooth base. This is the secret to factory-smooth finish.
🎨
Step 6
Apply 2-3 thin coats of cabinet enamel with foam roller and brush. Thin coats dry faster and level better than thick coats.
🔨
Step 7
Reinstall doors and hardware after 24-48 hours of cure time. Full hardness takes 7 days — be gentle until then.

Tip: Use cabinet-specific enamel (not wall paint) — it's harder, more washable, and resists chipping. Sherwin Williams ProClassic or Benjamin Moore Advance are pro favorites.

Caulk a Tub or Shower

● Easy ⏱️ 30 min 💰 $10-20
▼

Old caulk gets moldy, cracks, and lets water leak into walls. Recaulking every 2-3 years prevents water damage that costs thousands to repair.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🔫 Caulk Gun 🧴 Silicone Caulk 🔪 Caulk Removal Tool 🎗️ Painter's Tape
🛒 Shop Bathroom Caulk
⚠️ Safety First: Use 100% silicone caulk for wet areas — not acrylic or latex. Acrylic caulk is easier to apply but fails in 6-12 months in showers.
🔪
Step 1
Remove old caulk with removal tool or razor scraper. Get every bit — new caulk won't stick to old residue.
🧽
Step 2
Clean gap with rubbing alcohol — remove all soap scum and residue. Caulk needs clean, dry surface to adhere.
🎨
Step 3
Apply painter's tape 1/8 inch from gap on both sides. This creates perfect edges — pros use this trick.
✂️
Step 4
Cut caulk tube tip at 45° — opening size = gap width. Start small — you can always cut bigger.
🔫
Step 5
Apply steady bead of caulk, pulling gun along gap (don't push). Pulling gives smoother bead. Keep pressure consistent.
👆
Step 6
Smooth with wet finger or caulk finishing tool in one continuous motion. Have paper towels ready — caulk is messy.
✂️
Step 7
Remove tape immediately while caulk is wet — pull away from bead. Wait 1-2 hours, then use shower after 24 hours.

Tip: For perfectly straight caulk lines, use painter's tape on both sides of the gap. Apply caulk, smooth, remove tape immediately. Result looks like a pro did it — because pros DO do it this way.

Prep Walls for Painting

beginner ⏱️ 2-4 hours 💰 $20-40

Professional-looking paint jobs start with proper prep. Clean, patch, and prime for a flawless finish.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪣 Spackling Paste $5-10
  • 🔪 Putty Knife $5
  • 📄 Sandpaper $5
  • 🧴 TSP Cleaner $8
  • 🎀 Painter's Tape $5-10
⚠️ Safety First: If your home was built before 1978, test for lead paint before sanding. Lead dust is toxic, especially to children. Use a certified lead test kit.
  1. 🧹 Remove furniture or move it to the center and cover with drop cloths.
  2. 🧴 Wash walls with TSP or mild detergent to remove grease, dust, and grime — paint won't stick to dirty walls.
  3. 🔍 Inspect for holes, cracks, and nail pops. Mark them with painter's tape.
  4. 🪣 Fill holes and cracks with spackling paste, let dry 2-4 hours, then sand smooth with 120-grit paper.
  5. 🎀 Apply painter's tape to trim, ceilings, and areas you don't want to paint. Press edges firmly to prevent bleed-through.
  6. 🧹 Vacuum or dust the room thoroughly — loose dust will stick to wet paint and ruin the finish.

💡 Pro Tip: For glossy surfaces (tile, cabinets, trim), scuff-sand with 220-grit paper and apply a bonding primer — regular paint won't adhere to high-gloss without it.

Paint a Ceiling

beginner ⏱️ 3-4 hours 💰 $30-60

Ceiling painting is awkward but straightforward. Use the right technique to avoid lap marks and neck strain.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪣 Ceiling Paint $25-40
  • 🪄 Extension Pole $10-20
  • 🌀 Roller Cover $8-15
  • 🛡️ Drop Cloth $10
  • 🥽 Safety Glasses
⚠️ Safety First: Don't use regular wall paint on ceilings — it has lower viscosity and will drip. Ceiling paint is thicker and designed to minimize splatter.
  1. 🛡️ Cover everything — ceiling paint drips more than wall paint. Use canvas drop cloths, not plastic (slippery).
  2. 🥽 Wear safety glasses — paint in the eye is painful and dangerous.
  3. 🪣 Cut in around edges with a brush — ceiling meets walls at awkward angles, so take your time.
  4. 🌀 Roll in sections about 4-5 feet square. Work quickly to maintain a 'wet edge' and prevent lap marks.
  5. 🪄 Use an extension pole — standing on the floor is safer and less tiring than constantly moving a ladder.
  6. ⏳ Most ceilings need 2 coats. Let the first coat dry 4 hours before recoating.

💡 Pro Tip: Paint ceilings before walls — if you drip on the walls, you'll cover it when you paint the walls later. The reverse is not true.

Paint Trim and Baseboards

intermediate ⏱️ 4-6 hours 💰 $40-80

Crisp, clean trim makes rooms look finished. The secret is high-quality paint, good prep, and patience.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪣 Trim Paint $30-50
  • 🖌️ Angled Brush $10-20
  • 🎀 Painter's Tape $5-10
  • 📄 Sandpaper $5
  • 🧻 Tack Cloth $3
⚠️ Safety First: Semi-gloss and high-gloss paints show every brush stroke and imperfection. If your trim is damaged or uneven, consider satin finish instead.
  1. 🧹 Clean trim thoroughly — grease and dust prevent adhesion. Use TSP or degreaser on kitchen trim.
  2. 📄 Lightly sand trim with 220-grit paper to roughen the surface. Glossy finishes need scuffing.
  3. 🧻 Wipe dust with a tack cloth — even tiny particles show through paint.
  4. 🎀 Apply painter's tape to walls and floors along the trim edge. Press firmly with a putty knife for a seal.
  5. 🖌️ Paint with a 2-2.5 inch angled brush. Load 1/3 of the bristles, apply with long smooth strokes.
  6. ⏳ Remove tape while paint is slightly tacky (1-2 hours) for the cleanest edge — waiting until fully dry causes peeling.

💡 Pro Tip: For ultra-crisp lines, paint the wall color over the tape edge first, let dry, then paint the trim. Any bleed-through is the wall color, not visible.

Paint Kitchen Cabinets

intermediate ⏱️ 2-3 days 💰 $100-200

Cabinet painting transforms a kitchen for under $200. Proper prep and patience are essential — rushed work looks terrible.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪣 Cabinet Paint $50-100
  • 🧴 Deglosser $10
  • 🪣 Primer $20-30
  • 🌀 Foam Roller $5
  • 🪛 Screwdriver
⚠️ Safety First: Don't skip the primer step. Paint peeling off cabinets within months is almost always due to inadequate prep or skipping primer.
  1. 🪛 Remove all doors, drawers, and hardware. Label everything with tape for easy reassembly.
  2. 🧴 Clean all surfaces with TSP or degreaser — kitchen grease will ruin paint adhesion.
  3. 🧴 Degloss or sand the finish — shiny surfaces need roughing for paint to stick. Use liquid deglosser or 120-grit sandpaper.
  4. 🪣 Apply bonding primer. This is critical — regular primer won't adhere to cabinets. Let dry 24 hours.
  5. 🪣 Apply 2-3 thin coats of cabinet paint with a foam roller (smooth finish) or brush (traditional finish).
  6. ⏳ Let cure fully for 7 days before heavy use — paint feels dry in hours but isn't hard for a week.

💡 Pro Tip: Use a cabinet-specific paint (Benjamin Moore Advance, Sherwin-Willams Emerald Urethane) — they level beautifully and cure to a hard, washable finish.

Paint Exterior Trim

intermediate ⏱️ 1-2 days 💰 $100-200

Freshen your home's curb appeal by painting exterior trim, doors, and shutters. Do it right to withstand weather.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪣 Exterior Paint $40-80
  • 🔫 Caulk $5-10
  • ⛏️ Scraper $5-10
  • 🖌️ Wire Brush $5
  • 🪜 Ladder
⚠️ Safety First: Never paint over wet or damp wood — trapped moisture causes paint failure and wood rot. After rain or washing, wait at least 48 hours.
  1. 🌤️ Check weather — paint needs 24 hours of dry weather above 50°F. Avoid direct hot sun which causes blistering.
  2. ⛏️ Scrape loose paint with a scraper and wire brush — new paint over loose old paint will peel within a year.
  3. 🧴 Clean surfaces with a mix of TSP and water, or pressure wash on low setting. Let dry 24 hours.
  4. 🔫 Caulk gaps between trim and siding, and any cracks. Use paintable exterior caulk, not silicone.
  5. 🪣 Prime bare wood with exterior primer. Spot-prime scraped areas — full prime only if stripping to bare wood.
  6. 🖌️ Apply 2 coats of exterior paint with a brush for trim, roller for flat surfaces. Work top to bottom to catch drips.

💡 Pro Tip: For doors, paint the panels first, then the rails and stiles. Remove the door if possible — horizontal painting is easier and drips less.
