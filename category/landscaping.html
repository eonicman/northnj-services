Mow Your Lawn Like a Pro

● Easy ⏱️ 45 min 💰 $0
▼

The #1 lawn care mistake is cutting too short. Raise your mower deck to 3-3.5 inches for thicker, greener grass with fewer weeds and less watering.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🚜 Lawn Mower 🧵 String Trimmer 🍃 Leaf Blower
🛒 Shop Lawn Care Tools
⚠️ Safety First: Clear the lawn of sticks, stones, and toys before mowing. Wear closed-toe shoes and eye protection — mower blades can throw debris at 200+ mph.
☀️
Step 1
Mow when grass is dry — morning dew or rain causes clumping and uneven cuts. Wet grass also slips under the blade, giving a ragged cut.
📏
Step 2
Set mower deck to 3-3.5 inches (raise it higher than you think). Taller grass = deeper roots = drought resistance.
🔪
Step 3
Sharpen your mower blade — a dull blade tears grass, inviting disease. Sharpen every 20 hours of mowing or once per season.
🔄
Step 4
Mow in a different direction each time to prevent ruts and soil compaction. Alternate: horizontal, vertical, diagonal patterns weekly.
✂️
Step 5
Trim edges and borders with a string trimmer for a clean, professional look. Hold trimmer at slight angle for tapered edge.
🍂
Step 6
Leave clippings on the lawn — they return nitrogen to the soil. Only bag if grass is overgrown (clippings too thick).
💧
Step 7
Water deeply after mowing if rain isn't expected within 24 hours. 1 inch per week, including rainfall. Morning watering = less evaporation.

Tip: Never cut more than 1/3 of the grass blade height at once. Cutting too short shocks the grass and exposes soil, letting weeds germinate.

Edge Your Plant Beds

● Easy ⏱️ 30 min 💰 $0-20
▼

Clean bed edges instantly upgrade your landscape from 'meh' to 'magazine-worthy.' This free upgrade takes 30 minutes and makes mulch look intentional, not accidental.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🌙 Half-Moon Edger ⛏️ Garden Spade 🏠 Rubber Mulch Edging
🛒 Shop Edging Tools
⚠️ Safety First: Watch for buried sprinkler lines, electrical cables, or gas pipes. Call 811 before digging if you're unsure what's underground.
📍
Step 1
Mark your edge line with a garden hose or spray paint. Lay hose in curves for organic, natural-looking beds.
✂️
Step 2
Cut along the line with a half-moon edger — push down, rock back and forth. Cut 4-6 inches deep for clean, lasting edge.
🧱
Step 3
Remove the cut sod strip and compost it or use to patch bare spots. Shake off excess soil back into the bed first.
🎨
Step 4
Shape the bed edge into a gentle curve — avoid tight turns. Wide curves are easier to mow around and look more natural.
🛡️
Step 5
Install optional edging material if you want a permanent barrier. Metal edging = invisible, plastic = cheap, stone = premium.
🍂
Step 6
Mulch the bed to 2-3 inches deep, keeping mulch off plant stems. Mulch touching stems causes rot and invites pests.

Tip: For straight edges, use a board as a guide. For curves, a garden hose is the best layout tool — it shows you the exact shape before you cut.

Plant Shrubs & Trees

● Medium ⏱️ 1-2 hours 💰 $50-300
▼

Proper planting technique determines whether your tree lives 50 years or dies in 2. The most common mistake is planting too deep — roots need oxygen.

🛠️ Tools Needed

⛏️ Shovel 🔱 Garden Fork 🪵 Tree Stakes 🍂 Mulch
🛒 Shop Tree Planting Tools
⚠️ Safety First: Call 811 before digging holes deeper than 12 inches. Underground utilities are closer than you think — hitting a gas line is a $10,000+ mistake and life-threatening.
📞
Step 1
Call 811 or visit call811.com to mark underground utilities (free). Wait 2-3 business days for markers before digging.
⛏️
Step 2
Dig hole 2-3x wider than the root ball, but only as deep. Wide hole = loose soil for roots to spread. Deep hole = suffocation.
📏
Step 3
Set tree in hole — root flare (where trunk widens) should be visible above ground. Planting too deep is the #1 cause of tree death.
🏗️
Step 4
Backfill with native soil — don't amend with compost or potting mix. Amended soil acts like a sponge, drowning roots in wet conditions.
💧
Step 5
Water thoroughly to settle soil and eliminate air pockets. Slow, deep watering is better than frequent light sprinkles.
🍂
Step 6
Add 2-3 inch mulch ring extending to drip line, keeping 2 inches from trunk. Mulch volcano (piled against trunk) causes rot and bark death.
🪵
Step 7
Stake only if needed (windy area) — remove after 1 year. Staking too long prevents trunk from strengthening naturally.

Tip: Fall is the best time to plant trees and shrubs in most climates. Cool air + warm soil = root growth without the stress of summer heat.

Overseed Your Lawn

● Easy ⏱️ 2 hours 💰 $30-80
▼

Overseeding fills bare patches, thickens thin areas, and introduces newer grass varieties. Fall is ideal, but spring works too with proper watering.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🌾 Lawn Seed 🎯 Broadcast Spreader 🍂 Rake ⏰ Sprinkler Timer
🛒 Shop Grass Seed
⚠️ Safety First: Check seed label for sun/shade requirements — planting sun-loving seed in shade (or vice versa) wastes money and effort.
🌿
Step 1
Mow lawn shorter than usual (2 inches) and bag clippings. Short grass lets seed reach soil instead of getting caught in blades.
🍂
Step 2
Dethatch or rake vigorously to expose soil. Seed needs soil contact to germinate — it won't grow on thatch or dead grass.
🌾
Step 3
Spread seed with broadcast spreader — follow rate on bag (usually 4-6 lbs per 1000 sq ft). More is NOT better — overcrowded seedlings compete and die.
🍂
Step 4
Lightly rake to press seed into soil — 1/4 inch deep is ideal. Seed too deep won't germinate; on top gets eaten by birds.
💧
Step 5
Water 2-3x daily for 10-15 minutes until germination (7-14 days). Keep soil consistently moist, not soggy.
🌿
Step 6
First mow when new grass reaches 3 inches — use sharp blade, bag clippings. Wait until grass is established enough to handle the cut.

Tip: Buy premium seed with 0% weed seed and high germination rate (85%+). Cheap seed is mostly filler and annual ryegrass that dies in summer.

Build a Raised Garden Bed

● Medium ⏱️ 2-3 hours 💰 $50-150
▼

Raised beds solve poor soil, drainage issues, and back pain from bending. A 4x8 foot bed produces more vegetables than 100 sq ft of ground garden.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🪵 Cedar Boards 🔩 Drill 📐 Level 🛡️ Weed Barrier
🛒 Shop Raised Bed Kits
⚠️ Safety First: Use untreated cedar or redwood — pressure-treated lumber contains chemicals that leach into soil and vegetables. Composite lumber is safe but expensive.
📍
Step 1
Choose location with 6+ hours of sun and flat ground. Southern exposure is ideal — morning sun dries dew, reducing disease.
🛡️
Step 2
Lay weed barrier fabric on ground inside bed footprint. Staple to ground or weigh down with rocks — prevents grass from growing up through bed.
🔨
Step 3
Assemble 4x4 or 4x8 frame using corner brackets or screws. Pre-drill holes to prevent cedar from splitting.
📐
Step 4
Place frame and level with shims or dig down high corners. A level bed prevents water from pooling on one side.
🏗️
Step 5
Fill with mix: 60% topsoil, 30% compost, 10% perlite or vermiculite. Buy in bulk from landscape supply — cheaper than bagged.
💧
Step 6
Water thoroughly and let settle for 1 week before planting. Soil will compact — top off with more mix before planting.

Tip: Make beds no wider than 4 feet — you need to reach the center from either side without stepping in. Stepping on soil compacts it, destroying the drainage you just built.

Aerate Your Lawn

beginner ⏱️ 2-4 hours 💰 $50-80 (rental)

Compacted soil chokes grass roots. Annual aeration opens the soil for water, nutrients, and oxygen.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🌱 Core Aerator $50-80/day
  • 🚩 Lawn Markers
  • 💧 Watering Can
⚠️ Safety First: Never aerate when soil is soggy — you'll create muddy ruts instead of clean cores. The soil should be moist but not wet.
  1. 💧 Water lawn thoroughly 1-2 days before aerating — soft soil pulls better cores.
  2. 🚩 Mark sprinkler heads, buried cables, and shallow pipes with flags to avoid damage.
  3. 🌱 Run the aerator over the lawn in parallel passes, overlapping slightly.
  4. 🌱 Make a second pass perpendicular to the first for thorough coverage.
  5. 💧 Leave soil plugs on the lawn — they'll break down naturally and top-dress the soil.
  6. 🌱 Apply fertilizer and seed immediately after aerating — holes are perfect receptacles.

💡 Pro Tip: Fall is the best time to aerate cool-season grasses (fescue, bluegrass). Warm-season grasses (Bermuda, zoysia) prefer spring aeration.

Mulch a Garden Bed

beginner ⏱️ 1-2 hours 💰 $30-100

Proper mulch reduces weeds, retains moisture, and regulates soil temperature. A 2-3 inch layer is ideal.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪵 Mulch $30-100
  • 🛒 Wheelbarrow
  • ⛏️ Shovel
  • 🪄 Rake
  • 🛡️ Weed Barrier $15-30
⚠️ Safety First: Never pile mulch against tree trunks — it traps moisture, breeds fungus, and suffocates bark. Leave a 3-inch gap.
  1. 🌿 Remove weeds and debris from the bed — mulch won't stop established weeds.
  2. 🛡️ Optional: Lay landscape fabric to block weeds (skip if you want self-seeding plants).
  3. 📏 Calculate mulch needed: length × width × 0.25 feet (3 inches) ÷ 27 = cubic yards.
  4. 🪵 Pour mulch in small piles around the bed, then spread evenly with a rake.
  5. 🌳 Keep mulch 2-3 inches away from tree trunks and plant stems — 'volcano mulching' causes rot.
  6. 💧 Water thoroughly after mulching to settle it and prevent wind displacement.

💡 Pro Tip: Shredded hardwood mulch breaks down and enriches soil over time. Dyed mulches (red, black) are mainly decorative and don't improve soil.

Prune Small Trees and Shrubs

intermediate ⏱️ 1-3 hours 💰 $20-60

Proper pruning improves health, shape, and flowering. Done wrong, it damages or kills the plant.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • ✂️ Bypass Pruners $15-40
  • 🪚 Loppers $20-50
  • 🪚 Pruning Saw $20-40
  • 🧴 Rubbing Alcohol
⚠️ Safety First: Never 'top' trees (cut main trunk flat across) — it causes weak, dangerous regrowth and can kill the tree. Hire an arborist for large tree work.
  1. 🧴 Sanitize tools with rubbing alcohol between plants — prevents spreading disease.
  2. 🌳 Remove dead, damaged, or diseased branches first — cut back to healthy wood.
  3. 🌳 Remove crossing branches that rub against each other — they create wounds.
  4. 🌳 Thin dense areas to improve air circulation — remove weak or poorly positioned branches.
  5. ✂️ Make cuts at a 45° angle just above an outward-facing bud or branch collar (the swollen area where branch meets trunk).
  6. 🌳 Step back frequently to assess shape — don't remove more than 25% of canopy in one year.

💡 Pro Tip: Most trees and shrubs should be pruned in late winter before spring growth starts. Spring bloomers (forsythia, azalea) should be pruned after flowering.

Install Sod for Instant Lawn

intermediate ⏱️ 4-6 hours 💰 $300-800

Sod gives you an instant lawn without waiting for seed. Proper prep and installation are critical for success.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🌿 Sod $300-800
  • 🚜 Rototiller $50-80/day
  • 🛞 Lawn Roller $20-40/day
  • 🔪 Knife
  • 🧪 Starter Fertilizer $15-25
⚠️ Safety First: Sod is heavy (about 15 lbs per piece) and time-sensitive. Have help ready and don't order more than you can lay in one day.
  1. 🚜 Remove old grass/weeds, then rototill 4-6 inches deep to loosen soil.
  2. 🧪 Add 2-3 inches of compost and starter fertilizer, then rake smooth.
  3. 📏 Lay sod within 24 hours of delivery — it starts dying immediately if left stacked.
  4. 🌿 Start along a straight edge (driveway/walkway), lay strips tightly together like bricks.
  5. 🔪 Use a sharp knife to cut sod around curves, sprinkler heads, and obstacles.
  6. 💧 Water immediately and thoroughly — soak 6 inches deep. Keep moist for 2 weeks.

💡 Pro Tip: Buy 5-10% extra sod to account for cutting waste. Unused pieces can be composted or used for patch repairs later.

Build a Raised Garden Bed

beginner ⏱️ 2-4 hours 💰 $50-150

Raised beds improve drainage, reduce bending, and warm up faster in spring. Build one in an afternoon.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪵 Cedar Boards $50-100
  • 🔩 Screws $5
  • 🔩 Drill
  • 📐 Level
  • 🪴 Soil Mix $30-50
⚠️ Safety First: Never use pressure-treated lumber for vegetable beds — the chemicals leach into soil and food. Use cedar, redwood, or composite boards.
  1. 📏 Choose a sunny spot (6+ hours) and decide dimensions — 4ft wide is ideal for arm's reach from both sides.
  2. 🪵 Cut cedar boards to length. Cedar is rot-resistant and safe for edibles (avoid pressure-treated lumber).
  3. 🔩 Assemble the box with 3-inch exterior screws, pre-drilling to prevent splitting.
  4. 📐 Level the box using shims or by digging out high spots — water pooling kills plants.
  5. 🪴 Fill with quality soil mix: 50% topsoil, 30% compost, 20% peat moss or coconut coir.
  6. 🌱 Plant immediately or cover with mulch until ready — empty beds grow weeds fast.

💡 Pro Tip: Line the bottom with hardware cloth (metal mesh) to block gophers and voles from tunneling up into your bed.
