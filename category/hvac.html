Replace Your Thermostat

● Easy ⏱️ 30 min 💰 $50-150
▼

Upgrade to a programmable or smart thermostat to save 10-15% on heating/cooling bills.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🔧 Screwdriver Set ⚡ Voltage Tester 📋 Wire Labels 🔪 Utility Knife
🛒 Shop Your Thermostat
⚠️ Safety First: Turn off power to your HVAC system at the breaker before starting. Verify with a non-contact voltage tester.
Step 1
Turn off power at the breaker panel. Remove the old thermostat faceplate.
Step 2
Label wires with the provided stickers. R, W, Y, G, C — before disconnecting.
Step 3
Remove the old base by unscrewing from the wall. Keep screws if reusing.
Step 4
Install new base — use a level to ensure it's straight. Drill new holes if needed.
Step 5
Connect wires to matching terminals. Most modern thermostats have push-in connectors.
Step 6
Attach faceplate and power on at the breaker. You should hear a click.
Step 7
Configure settings — schedule, WiFi, preferences. Set your temperature schedule.

Tip: Take a photo of your old wiring before disconnecting — it's your safety net if you forget which wire goes where.

Change Your HVAC Air Filter

● Easy ⏱️ 5 min 💰 $10-40
▼

A clean filter improves air quality, reduces energy bills by 5-15%, and extends system life. Do this monthly during peak seasons.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🌬️ New Filter (correct size) 🔦 Flashlight
🛒 Shop Air Filter
⚠️ Safety First: Never run your system without a filter — dust will damage the blower motor.
  1. Locate your filter — usually in the return air duct, blower compartment, or behind a grille in a hallway.
  2. Note the arrow direction on the old filter (air flow direction) before removing.
  3. Check the size printed on the filter frame (e. g., 20x25x1).
  4. Slide out the old filter and dispose.
  5. Insert new filter with arrows pointing TOWARD the blower motor / furnace.
  6. Mark your calendar for the next change — monthly in summer/winter, every 2-3 months in spring/fall.

Tip: Filter size is printed on the cardboard frame (e.g., 20x25x1). Most common sizes: 20x25x1, 16x20x1, 20x20x1. Can't find it? Check the model number plate on your furnace — the manual lists the correct filter size.

Clean Your AC Condenser Coils

● Medium ⏱️ 45 min 💰 $15-30
▼

Dirty coils reduce efficiency by up to 30%. Annual cleaning keeps your AC running strong and prevents costly compressor damage.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🔧 Fin Comb 🧴 Foaming Cleaner 🚿 Garden Hose 🔧 Screwdriver 🧤 Work Gloves
🛒 Shop Condenser Coils
⚠️ Safety First: Never use a pressure washer — it will bend and damage the delicate aluminum fins.
  1. Turn off power to the outdoor unit at the disconnect box (usually a lever near the condenser).
  2. Remove the top grill/cage — typically 4-6 screws. Be careful not to damage wiring.
  3. Inspect coils for bent fins. Straighten with a fin comb by matching the fin spacing (8-24 fins per inch).
  4. Spray foaming cleaner generously on the coils. Let it sit 10-15 minutes.
  5. Rinse with gentle water pressure from inside out — spray through the coils toward the outside.
  6. Reassemble the cage and restore power. Allow 24 hours before evaluating performance improvement.

Tip: Do this in spring before the first heat wave, when HVAC techs are less busy if you need help.

Seal Leaky Air Ducts

● Medium ⏱️ 2-3 hours 💰 $25-60
▼

Leaky ducts waste 20-30% of conditioned air. Sealing them yourself pays for itself in one season through lower energy bills.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🧴 Mastic Sealant 📎 Foil Tape (UL181) 🔦 Flashlight 🧤 Work Gloves 😷 Dust Mask
🛒 Shop Air Ducts
⚠️ Safety First: Do NOT use standard gray duct tape — it dries out and fails within a year. Use mastic or UL181 foil tape only.
  1. Inspect accessible ductwork in basement, attic, or crawlspace. Look for gaps at joints, seams, and connections.
  2. Feel for air leaks while the system is running — a smoke pencil or incense stick makes leaks visible.
  3. Clean the area around leaks with a dry cloth so sealant will adhere.
  4. For small gaps (under 1/4"): Apply foil tape (UL181-rated, not regular duct tape which degrades).
  5. For larger gaps: Apply mastic sealant with a disposable brush — it's like thick paint that hardens permanently.
  6. Seal all register boots where ducts meet floor/wall/ceiling boots.
  7. Insulate ducts in unconditioned spaces (attic, crawlspace) with R-6 or higher duct insulation.

Tip: Focus on the supply ducts (blowing air into rooms) first — return duct leaks are less critical for efficiency.

Balance Room Temperatures

● Easy ⏱️ 20 min 💰 $0
▼

Rooms too hot or cold? Most imbalances are caused by closed vents, blocked returns, or incorrect dampers — all fixable without a service call.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🌡️ Infrared Thermometer 🪜 Step Stool
🛒 Shop Room Temperatures
⚠️ Safety First: Never close more than 20% of your vents — it creates back-pressure that strains the blower motor.
  1. Check that all supply vents are fully open, especially in the room that's too hot/cold.
  2. Ensure return air grilles aren't blocked by furniture, curtains, or rugs.
  3. Partially close (not fully block) vents in rooms that are too comfortable — this redirects airflow.
  4. Check for manual dampers in the basement on duct branches — they look like round handles on the ductwork.
  5. Adjust dampers seasonally: more airflow upstairs in summer (heat rises), more downstairs in winter.
  6. If one room is still off by 5+ degrees, you may have a duct leak or insulation issue — time to call a pro.

Tip: In summer, close basement vents 50% — basements are naturally cooler and need less AC.

Clean Your AC Condenser Coils

beginner ⏱️ 30 minutes 💰 $0

Dirty condenser coils reduce AC efficiency by up to 30%. Clean them yourself with a hose and fin comb.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🚿 Garden Hose
  • 🔧 Fin Comb $8-12
  • 🔫 Spray Nozzle
  • 🪛 Screwdriver
⚠️ Safety First: Never use a pressure washer — the high pressure will flatten the delicate aluminum fins permanently.
  1. 🚫 Turn OFF power to the outdoor unit at the breaker and disconnect box.
  2. 🔩 Remove the top grille or side panels to access the coils (usually 4-6 screws).

  4. 🚿 Spray coils from INSIDE out (not outside in) with moderate water pressure.
  5. 🧹 Straighten bent fins with a fin comb — work gently, one fin at a time.
  6. 📏 Check coils monthly during heavy use; clean at start of each season.

💡 Pro Tip: If you have a shop vac, use it to suck debris out from inside the unit before hosing.

Inspect Ductwork for Leaks

intermediate ⏱️ 45-60 minutes 💰 $15-30

Leaky ducts waste 20-30% of heated/cooled air. Find and seal them with mastic or foil tape.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🎀 Foil Tape $10-15
  • 🪣 Mastic Sealant $15-20
  • 🔦 Flashlight
  • 🌿 Incense Stick $3
⚠️ Safety First: Never use standard cloth duct tape — it fails within months. Use UL-181 rated foil tape only.
  1. 🔦 Turn on your HVAC fan (not heat or AC, just the fan) to pressurize the ducts.
  2. 🔍 Check all visible duct joints, connections, and seams in basement/crawlspace.
  3. 🌿 Hold an incense stick near joints — if smoke gets sucked in or blown away, there's a leak.
  4. 🎀 Seal small gaps (<1/4") with foil tape — press firmly, smooth all edges.
  5. 🪣 Apply mastic to larger gaps — it's messy but permanent. Wear gloves.
  6. 🔍 Check flex duct connections at boots and plenums — these are common failure points.

💡 Pro Tip: If you can access your main trunk line, look for disconnected flex ducts — they often fall off over time.

Maintain Your Whole-House Humidifier

beginner ⏱️ 20 minutes 💰 $25-40

A neglected humidifier breeds mold and bacteria. Clean it annually for healthy indoor air.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🧻 Humidifier Pads $20-30
  • 🍶 White Vinegar $3
  • 🖌️ Soft Brush
  • 🧹 Shop Vac
⚠️ Safety First: Never run a humidifier with a moldy pad — it will blow spores throughout your entire house.
  1. 🚫 Turn off power to the humidifier at the switch or breaker.
  2. 🔩 Open the humidifier panel — usually one or two thumb screws or latches.
  3. 🧻 Remove the old water panel/cartridge — note which direction the water flows.
  4. 🍶 Soak the distribution tray in vinegar for 10 minutes to dissolve mineral buildup.
  5. 🧹 Vacuum out any sediment or debris from the drain pan and housing.
  6. 🧻 Install the new pad with the colored dots facing UP (water flows down through it).

💡 Pro Tip: Write the replacement date on the new pad with a Sharpie so you know when it's due next year.

Install a Smart Thermostat

intermediate ⏱️ 45-60 minutes 💰 $150-250

Save 10-15% on energy bills with a programmable smart thermostat. Most homeowners can install it themselves.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 📱 Smart Thermostat $150-250
  • 🪛 Screwdriver Set
  • 🏷️ Wire Labels $5
  • 📐 Level
  • 🔩 Drill
⚠️ Safety First: If your system has thick wires (120V line voltage) instead of thin low-voltage wires, stop and call a pro. Smart thermostats only work with 24V low-voltage systems.
  1. 🚫 Turn OFF power to your HVAC system at the breaker — this is critical for safety.
  2. 🪛 Remove the old thermostat faceplate and take a photo of the wire configuration before disconnecting.
  3. 🏷️ Label each wire with the letter code (R, W, Y, G, C, etc.) — match letters exactly.
  4. 🔩 Remove the old mounting plate and install the new base, using a level to keep it straight.
  5. 🔗 Connect wires to matching terminals on the new thermostat (R→R, W→W, etc.).
  6. 📱 Turn power back on, follow the setup wizard, and connect to WiFi for remote control.

💡 Pro Tip: Most smart thermostats have a compatibility checker on their website — enter your wire letters before buying to confirm it will work with your system.

Relight Your Furnace Pilot Light

beginner ⏱️ 15 minutes 💰 $0

A blown-out pilot light is the #1 cause of 'no heat' calls. Relighting it yourself takes 5 minutes and saves a $150 service fee.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🔥 Long Match or Lighter
  • 🔦 Flashlight
⚠️ Safety First: If you smell gas, do NOT attempt to light the pilot. Turn off the gas valve, leave the house, and call the gas company from outside.
  1. 🚫 Turn the gas valve to OFF and wait 5 minutes for any gas to dissipate (smell for gas first).
  2. 🔦 Find the pilot assembly — it's a small tube near the burners with a knob labeled PILOT.
  3. 🔥 Turn the knob to PILOT and press down. Hold a lit match or long lighter at the pilot opening.
  4. 🔥 Keep the knob pressed for 30-60 seconds after the pilot lights to heat the thermocouple.
  5. 🔄 Release the knob slowly — if the pilot stays lit, turn the knob to ON.
  6. 🔥 If the pilot won't stay lit after 3 tries, the thermocouple may need replacement — call a pro.

💡 Pro Tip: Newer furnaces use electronic ignition (no standing pilot). If you don't see a pilot light, your furnace probably has a hot surface igniter — a different repair entirely.

💡 Pro Tip: Newer furnaces use electronic ignition (no standing pilot). If you don't see a pilot light, your furnace probably has a hot surface igniter — a different repair entirely.
