Inspect Your Roof for Damage

● Easy ⏱️ 30 min 💰 $0
▼

Catching roof problems early prevents $10,000+ water damage repairs. A 30-minute inspection twice a year (spring and fall) identifies issues before they become leaks.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🔭 Binoculars 🪜 Ladder 🧱 Roofing Cement
🛒 Shop Roof Repair
⚠️ Safety First: Use binoculars from the ground first. If you must go on the roof, use a safety harness tied to a chimney or vent stack. Never work on a wet or icy roof.
🔭
Step 1
Inspect from ground with binoculars — look for missing, curled, or cracked shingles. Also check for granules in gutters — means shingles are deteriorating.
🍂
Step 2
Check gutters and downspouts for shingle granules and debris. Excessive granules = shingles nearing end of life.
🛡️
Step 3
Inspect flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights for rust or gaps. Flashing is the #1 source of roof leaks.
🔍
Step 4
Check roof valleys — the V-shaped channels where two slopes meet. Valleys handle the most water flow and wear out first.
🏠
Step 5
Inspect attic for water stains, mold, or daylight showing through roof boards. These are signs of active leaks you haven't noticed yet.
📝
Step 6
Document findings with photos and note locations for future reference. This helps track deterioration over time.

Tip: If your roof is under 15 years old and you find minor issues, DIY repairs are cost-effective. If it's 20+ years old with widespread problems, start budgeting for full replacement ($8,000-15,000).

Replace Missing Shingles

● Medium ⏱️ 1-2 hours 💰 $30-60
▼

Missing shingles from wind or storm damage expose the underlayment and roof deck to water. Replacing them quickly prevents rot and interior damage.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🏠 Replacement Shingles 🔩 Roofing Nails 🔨 Hammer ⛏️ Pry Bar 🧱 Roofing Cement
🛒 Shop Replacement Shingles
⚠️ Safety First: Never work on a wet, icy, or windy day. Use a harness tied to a solid anchor. If the roof pitch is steeper than 6:12 (rise over run), hire a professional — falls are the leading cause of DIY roofing injuries.
🏠
Step 1
Match replacement shingle to existing — same brand, color, and style. Take a piece of old shingle to the store.
🔨
Step 2
Lift shingles above the damaged one with pry bar and remove nails. You need to access the nails holding the damaged shingle.
⬅️
Step 3
Slide out damaged shingle and remove all old nails from the area. Old nails will puncture the new shingle if left in.
➡️
Step 4
Slide new shingle into place, aligning with surrounding shingles. Make sure tabs line up with adjacent rows.
🔩
Step 5
Nail with 4 roofing nails — 2 above each cutout, 1 inch from edges. Nails should be covered by the shingle above (blind nailing).
🧱
Step 6
Apply roofing cement under lifted shingles above and press down firmly. This reseals the bond between shingles.
✅
Step 7
Check that new shingle lies flat and matches surrounding rows. A raised shingle will catch wind and tear off again.

Tip: Always replace shingles on a warm, sunny day (70°F+). Shingles are flexible when warm and seal better. Cold shingles crack when bent and won't seal properly.

Seal Roof Flashing

● Easy ⏱️ 45 min 💰 $15-30
▼

Flashing leaks are the #1 cause of roof water damage. Resealing with roofing cement stops leaks before they ruin ceilings, walls, and insulation.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🧱 Roofing Cement 🔫 Caulk Gun 🧹 Wire Brush 🔪 Putty Knife
🛒 Shop Flashing Repair
⚠️ Safety First: Work on a dry day with no rain forecast for 24 hours. Roofing cement needs time to cure. A sudden rain will wash uncured cement into your gutters — and your hair.
🔍
Step 1
Inspect flashing around chimney, vents, and valleys for gaps or rust. Look for lifted edges, cracked sealant, and corrosion.
🧹
Step 2
Clean area with wire brush — remove old caulk, dirt, and loose rust. Roofing cement only bonds to clean, dry metal.
🌞
Step 3
Wipe with cloth and let dry completely — moisture prevents adhesion. Use a heat gun on low if needed to speed drying.
🧱
Step 4
Apply generous bead of roofing cement along gap with caulk gun. Don't skimp — a thin layer cracks in months.
🪒
Step 5
Spread cement 2-3 inches beyond gap with putty knife for waterproof seal. Feather edges into surrounding surface.
🕸️
Step 6
For large gaps: embed fiberglass mesh in cement, then coat over it. Mesh prevents cracking as metal expands/contracts.
⏰
Step 7
Let cure 24 hours before exposing to rain — mark calendar to check annually. Set phone reminder: 'Check roof flashing' every spring.

Tip: For chimney flashing, inspect the step flashing where it meets the brick. Mortar cracks are common here — seal with masonry caulk, not roofing cement, for best adhesion to brick.

Clean Moss Off Roof

● Easy ⏱️ 2 hours 💰 $20-40
▼

Moss holds moisture against shingles, accelerating decay and lifting edges that let water in. Remove it before it causes permanent damage requiring full roof replacement.

🛠️ Tools Needed

☠️ Moss Killer 🧹 Stiff Brush 🚿 Garden Hose 🔗 Safety Harness
🛒 Shop Moss Prevention
⚠️ Safety First: Never pressure wash asphalt shingles — it strips protective granules and voids warranties. Use gentle scrubbing and chemical treatment only.
☠️
Step 1
Apply moss killer spray on dry day — follow product directions. Most contain zinc sulfate — kills moss in 2-3 weeks.
⏳
Step 2
Wait 2-3 weeks for moss to turn brown and die. Patience here — dead moss removes much easier than live.
🧹
Step 3
Gently brush dead moss off with stiff brush — work downward. Don't scrub hard — you're just removing dead moss, not cleaning the shingle.
💧
Step 4
Rinse with garden hose on gentle spray — not pressure washer. Let gravity do the work — start at peak, work down.
🍂
Step 5
Clean gutters after — they'll be full of moss debris. Wet moss is heavy — don't let gutters sag.
🛡️
Step 6
Install zinc or copper strips at roof peak — rain washes ions down, preventing regrowth. Lasts 10-20 years. Professional secret for moss prevention.

Tip: For persistent moss, install zinc strips (or slip a few pennies under shingles at the peak). Rain dissolves tiny amounts of zinc, creating an environment moss can't grow in. It's passive, long-term prevention.

Install Gutter Guards

● Easy ⏱️ 2-3 hours 💰 $100-300
▼

Gutter guards reduce cleaning from 2-3x/year to once every 2-3 years. They also prevent clogs that cause overflow, foundation damage, and basement flooding.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🕸️ Gutter Guard Mesh ✂️ Tin Snips 🔩 Screws 🔩 Drill
🛒 Shop Gutter Guards
⚠️ Safety First: Use a sturdy ladder with standoff arms that rest on the roof, not the gutter. Gutters aren't designed to support ladder weight and will bend or detach.
🍂
Step 1
Clean gutters thoroughly before installation — remove all debris. Guards trap existing debris if not cleaned first.
📏
Step 2
Measure gutter length and buy guards with 10% extra for cutting waste. Standard K-style gutters are 5 or 6 inches wide.
✂️
Step 3
Cut guards to length with tin snips — wear gloves, edges are sharp. Cut slightly short (1/4 inch) to allow expansion.
⬆️
Step 4
Slide guards under shingles and rest front edge on gutter lip. Most mesh guards slide under first course of shingles.
🔩
Step 5
Secure with self-tapping screws through guard into gutter front edge. Space screws every 2 feet for wind resistance.
💧
Step 6
Run hose on roof and verify water flows into gutter, not over the edge. If water overshoots, guards are too flat — adjust angle.

Tip: Avoid foam and brush-style gutter inserts — they trap seeds and become nurseries for plants. Stainless steel micro-mesh is the best long-term solution. It's more expensive upfront but pays for itself in reduced cleaning and maintenance.

Clean Your Gutters

beginner ⏱️ 2-3 hours 💰 $0-30

Clogged gutters cause water damage, foundation problems, and ice dams. Clean them twice a year for protection.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪜 Ladder
  • 🧤 Gloves
  • ⛏️ Trowel
  • 🪣 Bucket
  • 🚿 Hose
⚠️ Safety First: Never lean the ladder against gutters — they can't support your weight and will dent or pull away from the fascia.
  1. 🪜 Set ladder on firm, level ground. Use a ladder stabilizer or standoff to protect gutters from denting.
  2. 🧤 Wear heavy work gloves — gutters contain rotting leaves, animal droppings, and sharp metal edges.
  3. ⛏️ Scoop debris into a bucket with a gutter trowel or small garden shovel. Work toward the downspout.
  4. 🚿 Flush gutters with a hose after scooping — check that water flows freely through downspouts.
  5. 🔍 Check for sagging sections, loose hangers, and leaks at seams. Mark problem areas.
  6. 🔧 Tighten loose hangers and repair leaks with gutter sealant — clean and dry the area first.

💡 Pro Tip: Install gutter guards to reduce cleaning frequency. Mesh or screen types work best — foam inserts can cause more problems than they solve.

Inspect Your Roof

beginner ⏱️ 30-60 minutes 💰 $0

Catch roof problems early before they cause interior damage. Inspect from the ground, ladder, and attic.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🔭 Binoculars
  • 🪜 Ladder
  • 🔦 Flashlight
  • 📷 Camera
⚠️ Safety First: Never walk on a wet, icy, or steep roof. If you can't safely inspect from a ladder, hire a professional. Falls from roofs are deadly.
  1. 🔭 From the ground, use binoculars to check for missing, curled, or damaged shingles.
  2. 🔍 Look for moss, algae, or dark streaks — they trap moisture and accelerate shingle deterioration.
  3. 🪜 Check flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights from a ladder — these are the #1 leak sources.
  4. 🏠 Inspect the attic for water stains, mold, or daylight showing through the roof boards.
  5. 📷 Document everything with photos — you'll need them for insurance claims or contractor quotes.
  6. 📝 If you find damage, get 3 quotes from licensed roofers. Small repairs ($200-500) can prevent major replacements ($10,000+).

💡 Pro Tip: Check your roof after every major storm — high winds and hail cause damage that isn't visible from the ground. Most insurance covers storm damage if reported promptly.

Patch a Roof Leak

intermediate ⏱️ 1-2 hours 💰 $20-50

A small leak can cause thousands in interior damage. Patch it temporarily until a roofer can make permanent repairs.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪣 Roofing Cement $10-15
  • 🛡️ Roofing Patch $15-25
  • 🔪 Putty Knife
  • 🔫 Caulk Gun
  • 🪜 Ladder
⚠️ Safety First: Roof work is dangerous. If you're uncomfortable on a ladder or the roof is steep (more than 6/12 pitch), call a professional. No leak is worth a fall.
  1. 🏠 Find the leak source inside first — trace water stains uphill to where water enters.
  2. 🪜 Set up ladder safely. Go up during dry weather — wet roofs are extremely dangerous.
  3. 🔍 Look for damaged shingles, cracked flashing, or gaps around vents and chimneys near the interior leak.
  4. ⛏️ Lift damaged shingles carefully and apply roofing cement underneath with a putty knife.
  5. 🛡️ For larger holes, cut a roofing patch to size, cement around the hole, press patch firmly, then cement over the top.
  6. ⏳ This is a temporary fix. Schedule a roofer within 2-4 weeks for permanent repair or replacement.

💡 Pro Tip: For emergency interior leaks, place a bucket and poke a small hole in the ceiling drywall where water is pooling — it prevents the water from spreading across the ceiling.

Improve Attic Ventilation

intermediate ⏱️ 2-4 hours 💰 $50-200

Poor attic ventilation causes ice dams, mold, and high cooling bills. Balance intake and exhaust airflow for a healthy roof.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🌬️ Soffit Vents $5-15 each
  • 🌀 Attic Fan $100-200
  • 📐 Baffles $2-5 each
  • 🌡️ Thermometer
  • 😷 Dust Mask
⚠️ Safety First: Never block ventilation in winter thinking it will save heat — trapped moisture causes mold, rot, and ice dams that cost far more than the heat loss.
  1. 🌡️ Check attic temperature on a hot day — it should be within 10-15°F of outside temperature. Higher means poor ventilation.
  2. 🌬️ Verify soffit vents (intake) aren't blocked by insulation — install baffles to keep channels open.
  3. 🌀 Check exhaust vents (ridge, gable, or roof vents) for bird nests, debris, or damage.
  4. 📏 Calculate ventilation: you need 1 sq ft of vent per 150 sq ft of attic floor (or 1:300 with vapor barrier).
  5. 🌬️ Add soffit vents if intake is insufficient — use a hole saw from below or have a pro install them.
  6. 🌀 Install an attic fan if passive ventilation isn't enough — thermostatically controlled models run automatically.

💡 Pro Tip: Ridge vents combined with soffit vents create natural convection — cool air enters low, hot air exits high. This is the most effective passive ventilation system.

Maintain Skylights

beginner ⏱️ 1-2 hours 💰 $15-40

Skylights are beautiful but prone to leaks. Annual maintenance prevents water damage and extends their lifespan.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🔫 Silicone Caulk $5-10
  • 🧴 Glass Cleaner
  • 🧻 Microfiber Cloth
  • 🪜 Ladder
  • 🖌️ Soft Brush
⚠️ Safety First: Never use pressure washers on skylights — the force can break seals, force water inside, and crack the glass.
  1. 🧹 Clean interior and exterior glass with non-ammonia glass cleaner — ammonia can damage seals and coatings.
  2. 🔍 Inspect the flashing around the skylight from the roof. Look for cracked caulk, lifted seams, or rust.
  3. 🔫 Re-caulk any gaps with exterior-grade silicone — clean old caulk first, apply a smooth bead, tool with a wet finger.
  4. 🧹 Clear debris from the weep holes (small drains) around the frame — clogged weep holes trap water.
  5. 🔍 Check the interior frame for water stains, peeling paint, or mold — these indicate active leaks.
  6. 🪟 Open and close operable skylights to ensure mechanisms work smoothly. Lubricate hinges if sticky.

💡 Pro Tip: Apply a clear UV-protective film to south-facing skylights — it reduces heat gain in summer and prevents furniture fading without blocking light.
