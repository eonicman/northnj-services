Clean Gutters Safely

● Easy ⏱️ 1 hour 💰 $0-25
▼

Clogged gutters cause water damage, foundation problems, and ice dams. Cleaning them twice a year (spring and fall) prevents thousands in repair costs.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🪜 Ladder 🥄 Gutter Scoop 🚿 Garden Hose 🧤 Work Gloves
🛒 Shop Gutter Guards
⚠️ Safety First: Never lean over the side of the ladder — keep your belt buckle between the rails. Have a spotter hold the ladder base, especially on soft ground.
🪜
Step 1
Inspect ladder for damage and set on firm, level ground. Angle should be 75 degrees (1 foot out for every 4 feet up).
⬇️
Step 2
Start at the downspout and work away from it. This prevents pushing debris into the downspout and clogging it.
🥄
Step 3
Scoop out leaves, twigs, and sediment with gutter scoop or small trowel. Work in 2-3 foot sections — don't overreach.
🗑️
Step 4
Drop debris into bucket or tarp below, or use bucket hanger attached to ladder. A plastic bucket with wire hook works great.
💧
Step 5
Flush gutter with hose, working toward downspout. Check water flow — if it backs up, downspout is clogged.
🔧
Step 6
If downspout is clogged, run hose up from bottom or use plumber's snake. You can also try tapping the downspout to dislodge clogs.
✅
Step 7
Verify water flows freely through downspout to drainage point. If water pools near foundation, extend downspout 3+ feet away.

Tip: Install gutter guards to reduce cleaning frequency from 2x/year to once every 2-3 years. Mesh guards work best — foam inserts deteriorate and become debris themselves.

Pressure Wash Decks & Driveways

● Easy ⏱️ 2-3 hours 💰 $30-50 rental
▼

Pressure washing removes years of grime, mold, and stains in minutes. Rent a unit for $30-50/day — much cheaper than hiring a pro ($200-400).

🛠️ Tools Needed

🔫 Pressure Washer 🥽 Safety Goggles 🧴 Deck Cleaner ⭕ Spray Tip 25°
🛒 Shop Pressure Washers
⚠️ Safety First: Never point pressure washer at people, pets, or windows. The spray can cut skin and shatter glass. Wear closed-toe shoes — the recoil can knock you off balance.
🚧
Step 1
Clear the area of furniture, plants, and delicate items. Cover nearby plants with plastic sheeting if using cleaner.
🧴
Step 2
Apply deck or concrete cleaner and let sit for 10-15 minutes. Don't let it dry — mist with water if needed.
🔌
Step 3
Connect pressure washer to outdoor faucet using garden hose. Use a 3/4 inch or larger hose for adequate water flow.
💨
Step 4
Test spray pattern on inconspicuous area — start with wide fan tip (25°). Hold nozzle 8-12 inches from surface. Closer = more pressure = more damage risk.
🔄
Step 5
Work in sections, sweeping motion, overlapping each pass by 50%. Follow wood grain on decks to avoid streaks.
💦
Step 6
Rinse thoroughly from top to bottom (decks) or high to low (driveways). This prevents dirty water from running over cleaned areas.
🌞
Step 7
Let dry 48 hours before applying sealer or stain (decks only). Sealing wet wood traps moisture and causes rot.

Tip: For wood decks, use 1500-2000 PSI max. Higher pressure etches and damages wood fibers. For concrete, 2500-3000 PSI is safe and more effective.

Deep Clean Bathroom Grout

● Easy ⏱️ 1 hour 💰 $5-15
▼

Grout turns gray and discolored from soap scum, mildew, and hard water stains. Restore it to original color without re-grouting — a $5 fix vs $500+ replacement.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🪥 Grout Brush 🥫 Baking Soda 🍶 White Vinegar 💨 Spray Bottle
🛒 Shop Grout Sealers
⚠️ Safety First: Never mix bleach with vinegar or ammonia — it creates toxic chlorine gas. Pick one cleaner type and stick with it.
🪟
Step 1
Open windows and run exhaust fan for ventilation. Even natural cleaners need airflow in small bathrooms.
🥣
Step 2
Make paste: 3 parts baking soda + 1 part water. Should be thick like toothpaste — add more soda if too thin.
🖐️
Step 3
Apply paste to grout lines with old toothbrush or grout brush. Work in 2x2 foot sections so paste doesn't dry out.
💨
Step 4
Spray white vinegar over the baking soda paste — it will fizz. The fizzing reaction lifts dirt and kills mildew.
🪥
Step 5
Scrub grout lines with stiff brush for 2-3 minutes per section. Use circular motion — gets into porous grout better.
💦
Step 6
Rinse with warm water and dry with microfiber cloth. Check results — stubborn stains may need a second round.
🛡️
Step 7
Apply grout sealer after 24 hours of drying. Sealer prevents future staining — reapply annually.

Tip: For severe mold stains, use hydrogen peroxide (3%) instead of vinegar. Spray on, let sit 10 minutes, scrub, rinse. It's a stronger oxidizer than the baking soda/vinegar combo.

Remove Mold & Mildew

● Easy ⏱️ 30 min 💰 $3-10
▼

Mold in bathrooms, basements, and window sills is a health hazard. Kill it at the root (not just the surface) with the right cleaner and prevention steps.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🧪 Hydrogen Peroxide 💨 Spray Bottle 🪥 Scrub Brush 😷 N95 Mask
🛒 Shop Dehumidifiers
⚠️ Safety First: Wear an N95 mask and gloves when cleaning mold. Disturbed mold releases spores into the air. For areas larger than 10 square feet, call a professional mold remediator.
😷
Step 1
Put on N95 mask, gloves, and eye protection. Mold spores are allergenic — protect your lungs and eyes.
🧪
Step 2
Fill spray bottle with undiluted 3% hydrogen peroxide. Hydrogen peroxide kills mold roots — bleach only bleaches the color.
💨
Step 3
Saturate moldy area completely — let sit 10-15 minutes. It will bubble and fizz as it attacks the mold.
🪥
Step 4
Scrub with stiff brush until mold is gone. For porous surfaces (drywall, wood), you may need to remove the material if mold has penetrated.
🧽
Step 5
Wipe with damp cloth, then dry thoroughly. Moisture is mold's best friend — eliminate it.
🛡️
Step 6
Prevent return: run exhaust fan 30 min after showers, fix leaks, dehumidify to 50%. A $30 dehumidifier in the bathroom prevents 90% of mold issues.

Tip: If mold keeps returning in the same spot, you have a moisture problem behind the surface. Check for leaking pipes, condensation, or poor ventilation — the mold will keep winning until the moisture is fixed.

Clean Your Dryer Vent

● Easy ⏱️ 45 min 💰 $15-30
▼

Clogged dryer vents cause 2,900 house fires per year. Cleaning takes 45 minutes and reduces drying time by 50% — saving electricity and preventing a fire hazard.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🧹 Dryer Vent Brush 🌪️ Vacuum with Hose 🎗️ Foil Tape 🔩 Screwdriver
🛒 Shop Vent Cleaning Kits
⚠️ Safety First: Unplug the dryer before cleaning. Gas dryers: turn off gas valve too. The vent contains highly flammable lint — clean it regularly.
🔌
Step 1
Unplug dryer (electric) or turn off gas valve (gas dryers). Pull dryer away from wall carefully — don't kink the gas line.
🔧
Step 2
Disconnect vent hose from dryer and wall. Use screwdriver to loosen clamps. Check hose for damage — replace if cracked.
🌪️
Step 3
Vacuum lint from dryer opening, hose, and wall vent. Use vacuum hose attachment — get as deep as possible.
🧹
Step 4
Feed vent brush into wall duct and rotate to dislodge packed lint. Work from both ends if possible — outside vent may be easier access.
🚪
Step 5
Clean outside vent cover — remove flap and clear lint buildup. Check that flap moves freely — stuck flaps trap moist air.
🔗
Step 6
Reconnect with foil tape (not duct tape — it fails with heat). Seal all joints airtight to prevent lint escape into walls.
✅
Step 7
Run dryer on air fluff cycle and feel for strong airflow at outside vent. Weak airflow = remaining blockage.

Tip: Clean the lint trap after EVERY load, and deep-clean the vent annually. A clogged lint trap is annoying; a clogged vent is a fire waiting to happen. Mark your calendar.

Deep Clean Tile Grout

beginner ⏱️ 1-2 hours 💰 $10-20

Dirty grout makes clean tiles look terrible. Restore it with a simple DIY cleaner instead of expensive professional services.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🧂 Baking Soda $2
  • 🧪 Hydrogen Peroxide $2
  • 🖌️ Grout Brush $5-10
  • 🧴 Spray Bottle
  • 🛡️ Sealer $15-25
⚠️ Safety First: Never mix bleach with ammonia-based cleaners — it creates toxic chloramine gas. Use one or the other, never both.
  1. 🧹 Sweep or vacuum the floor thoroughly — loose dirt will muddy your cleaning solution.
  2. 🧂 Mix baking soda and hydrogen peroxide into a paste (about 2:1 ratio).
  3. 🧴 Apply paste to grout lines with a spray bottle or spoon, working in small sections.
  4. 🖌️ Scrub with a grout brush or old toothbrush — use circular motions and apply pressure.
  5. ⏳ Let sit for 10-15 minutes, then rinse with clean water and dry with a towel.
  6. 🛡️ Once fully dry (24 hours), apply grout sealer to prevent future staining — use a foam brush or applicator bottle.

💡 Pro Tip: For really stained grout, use an electric grout scrubber ($25-40) — it saves your wrists and cuts time in half.

Streak-Free Window Cleaning

beginner ⏱️ 30-60 minutes 💰 $5-15

Professional window cleaners charge $10-15 per window. Do it yourself with the right technique for crystal-clear results.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪟 Squeegee $10-20
  • 🧻 Microfiber Cloths $8-15
  • 🧴 Dish Soap
  • 🪣 Bucket
  • 🪄 Extension Pole $15-25
⚠️ Safety First: Never clean windows in direct sunlight — the solution dries too fast and leaves streaks. Choose an overcast day or work in shade.
  1. 🧹 Remove dust and cobwebs from frames and sills with a dry microfiber cloth first.
  2. 🧴 Mix 1 teaspoon dish soap per gallon of warm water — too much soap causes streaks.
  3. 🪟 Wet the entire window with a sponge or cloth soaked in the solution.
  4. 🪟 Starting at the top, pull the squeegee down in straight strokes, overlapping each pass by 1 inch.
  5. 🧻 Wipe the squeegee blade with a clean cloth after each stroke to prevent streak transfer.
  6. 🧻 Dry edges and corners with a microfiber cloth — these are where streaks hide.

💡 Pro Tip: For hard water spots, spray white vinegar on the glass, let sit 5 minutes, then squeegee. Vinegar dissolves mineral deposits without scrubbing.

Remove Carpet Stains

beginner ⏱️ 15-30 minutes 💰 $5-20

Most carpet stains can be removed with household products if treated quickly. The key is blotting, not rubbing.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🍶 White Vinegar
  • 🧴 Dish Soap
  • 🧂 Baking Soda
  • 🧻 Clean Cloths
  • 🧴 Spray Bottle
⚠️ Safety First: Never use hot water on protein stains (blood, milk, eggs) — it cooks the proteins into the fibers permanently.
  1. 🧻 Blot (don't rub!) the stain immediately with a clean cloth to absorb as much as possible.
  2. 🧴 For most stains: mix 1 tablespoon dish soap + 2 cups warm water. Apply sparingly with a spray bottle.
  3. 🧻 Blot from the outside of the stain inward to prevent spreading — use a clean cloth section each time.
  4. 💧 Rinse with plain water by spraying lightly and blotting — soap residue attracts dirt.
  5. 🧂 For odors: sprinkle baking soda on the area after cleaning, let dry overnight, then vacuum.
  6. 🌬️ Place a fan on the spot to speed drying — damp carpet breeds mold and mildew.

💡 Pro Tip: Keep a stain removal kit ready: white vinegar, dish soap, baking soda, hydrogen peroxide, and clean cloths in a basket under the sink.

Deep Clean Your Microwave

beginner ⏱️ 15 minutes 💰 $0

A steam-clean with lemon loosens baked-on food in 5 minutes. No scrubbing required.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🍋 Lemon
  • 🥣 Bowl
  • 🧻 Microfiber Cloth
  • 🧽 Sponge
⚠️ Safety First: Be careful removing the hot bowl — use oven mitts. The lemon water will be boiling.
  1. 🍋 Cut a lemon in half and squeeze juice into a microwave-safe bowl with 1 cup water.
  2. 🍋 Drop the squeezed lemon halves into the bowl too — the oils help cut grease.
  3. ⏱️ Microwave on high for 5 minutes. The steam will condense on the interior walls and loosen grime.
  4. ⏳ Let stand 2 more minutes without opening the door — the steam continues working.
  5. 🧻 Wipe the interior with a damp microfiber cloth — everything should wipe off easily.
  6. 🧽 Remove the turntable and wash it in the sink with regular dish soap.

💡 Pro Tip: For stubborn grease, add a tablespoon of white vinegar to the lemon water — the acid cuts through grease better than lemon alone.

Organize Your Garage

beginner ⏱️ 4-6 hours 💰 $100-300

A cluttered garage wastes space and creates hazards. Transform it with zones, shelving, and wall storage.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 📦 Shelving Units $50-150
  • 🔨 Pegboard $20-40
  • 🗂️ Storage Bins $30-60
  • 🚲 Bike Hooks $10-20
  • 🏷️ Label Maker $20-40
⚠️ Safety First: Never store propane tanks, paint cans, or chemicals in an attached garage without proper ventilation — fumes can seep into the house.
  1. 🗑️ Pull everything out and sort into keep, donate, sell, and trash piles. Be ruthless.
  2. 🧹 Sweep and clean the empty garage — this is your only chance to clean the floor completely.
  3. 📦 Install shelving along walls — keep heavy items low, light items high, frequently-used at eye level.
  4. 🔨 Mount pegboard for tools, or use slatwall/panel systems for heavier equipment.
  5. 🚲 Use vertical space: wall hooks for bikes, overhead racks for seasonal items, ceiling storage for bins.
  6. 🏷️ Label every bin and shelf zone — if you can't find it in 10 seconds, the system has failed.

💡 Pro Tip: Create zones: automotive, tools, sports, seasonal, and household. Everything gets a home zone. If it doesn't fit, it doesn't stay.
