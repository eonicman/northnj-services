Fix a Leaky Faucet

● Easy ⏱️ 20 min 💰 $5-15
▼

Stop that annoying drip and save up to 10% on your water bill. Most leaks are caused by worn washers or O-rings — a $2 fix.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🔧 Adjustable Wrench ⭕ Replacement Washers 🧴 Plumber's Grease 🔦 Flashlight
🛒 Shop Faucet Repair Kits
⚠️ Safety First: Turn off the water supply under the sink (clockwise). Open the faucet to release pressure before disassembling.
🔒
Step 1
Turn off water supply valves under the sink. Clockwise to close. Open faucet to drain remaining water.
🛑
Step 2
Plug the sink drain with a rag or stopper. Prevents small parts from falling down the drain.
🔧
Step 3
Remove the faucet handle — usually a set screw under the cap. Use an Allen wrench or flathead to pry off the cap first.
🔩
Step 4
Extract the cartridge or stem by unscrewing the retaining nut. Note the orientation — take a photo for reference.
⭕
Step 5
Replace the washer or O-ring at the base of the cartridge. Match the size exactly — bring the old one to the hardware store.
🔨
Step 6
Reassemble in reverse order — don't overtighten. Hand-tighten the retaining nut, then 1/4 turn with wrench.
✅
Step 7
Turn water back on and test — no drips? You're done! Check both hot and cold handles.

Tip: If you can't find the exact washer, buy an assorted pack. They're cheaper than a service call and you'll have spares for next time.

Unclog Any Drain

● Easy ⏱️ 15 min 💰 $0-25
▼

Hair, grease, and soap scum cause 90% of clogs. Learn to clear them without calling a plumber — or making it worse with chemical cleaners.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🪠 Plunger 🐍 Drain Snake 🥫 Baking Soda 🍶 White Vinegar
🛒 Shop Drain Cleaning Tools
⚠️ Safety First: Never use chemical drain cleaners before plunging — they can splash caustic liquid into your eyes. Try mechanical methods first.
🪠
Step 1
Plunge vigorously for 30 seconds — create a tight seal over the drain. For sinks, cover the overflow hole with a wet rag for better suction.
🥫
Step 2
Pour 1/2 cup baking soda down the drain, followed by 1/2 cup vinegar. It will fizz — that's the reaction breaking down organic matter.
☕
Step 3
Wait 15 minutes, then flush with boiling water. The heat melts grease that the baking soda/vinegar loosened.
🐍
Step 4
If still clogged, feed a drain snake into the pipe and twist to grab hair/debris. Pull out slowly — the clog usually comes with it.
🔍
Step 5
For stubborn clogs, remove and clean the P-trap under the sink. Place a bucket underneath — water will spill out.
💧
Step 6
Run water to confirm the drain flows freely. If it backs up again, the clog may be further down the line.

Tip: Pour boiling water down drains weekly as maintenance — it prevents grease buildup before it becomes a clog.

Replace a Toilet Flapper

● Easy ⏱️ 10 min 💰 $5-10
▼

A running toilet wastes 200+ gallons per day. Replacing the flapper is the #1 fix — takes 10 minutes and costs under $10.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🪶 Replacement Flapper 🧽 Sponge 🪣 Bucket
🛒 Shop Toilet Repair Kits
⚠️ Safety First: Turn off the water supply behind the toilet. Flush to drain the tank — sponge out remaining water to avoid spills.
🔒
Step 1
Turn off water supply behind toilet (clockwise). Flush to empty the tank.
🧽
Step 2
Sponge out remaining water from the tank. This prevents spills when you remove the old flapper.
⛓️
Step 3
Disconnect the chain from the flush handle lever. Note the chain length — you'll match it on the new flapper.
🪶
Step 4
Remove the old flapper from the overflow tube pegs. Most clip on — squeeze the sides and lift off.
✨
Step 5
Install new flapper onto the pegs, reconnect chain. Chain should have 1/2 inch slack when handle is at rest.
💦
Step 6
Turn water back on, let tank fill, and test flush. Listen — running water sound should stop within 30 seconds of fill.

Tip: If your toilet still runs after replacing the flapper, check the fill valve. A worn fill valve is the #2 cause of running toilets.

Fix Low Water Pressure

● Medium ⏱️ 30 min 💰 $10-50
▼

Weak shower? Slow-filling washing machine? Low pressure has 4 common causes — and 3 of them are DIY-fixable without a plumber.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🔧 Adjustable Wrench 🧹 Wire Brush 🍶 White Vinegar 🔗 Zip Ties
🛒 Shop Water Pressure Tools
⚠️ Safety First: If pressure dropped suddenly throughout the house, check for a main valve partially closed or a water main break in your area before troubleshooting fixtures.
🔍
Step 1
Remove and clean faucet aerators — unscrew the tip of the faucet. Soak in vinegar overnight to dissolve mineral buildup.
🚿
Step 2
Clean or replace the showerhead — most clogs are calcium deposits. Soak showerhead in vinegar bag tied with zip ties for 2 hours.
⚙️
Step 3
Verify all shutoff valves are fully open — especially after recent repairs. Counter-clockwise = open. Should turn freely until it stops.
📊
Step 4
Check the Pressure Reducing Valve (PRV) near your main shutoff. If the gauge reads below 40 PSI, adjust the screw clockwise to increase.
🔥
Step 5
Flush your water heater — sediment reduces hot water pressure. Connect garden hose to drain valve, run until water runs clear.
💧
Step 6
Check for hidden leaks — look for water meter movement when all fixtures are off. If meter spins, you have a leak. Call a pro for slab leaks.

Tip: Normal residential water pressure is 40-60 PSI. Above 80 PSI damages appliances; below 30 PSI gives weak showers.

Install an Under-Sink Water Filter

● Medium ⏱️ 45 min 💰 $50-200
▼

Remove chlorine, lead, and contaminants from your drinking water. Under-sink filters are easier to install than whole-house systems and cost less long-term than bottled water.

🛠️ Tools Needed

🔩 Drill 🔧 Adjustable Wrench 🎗️ Teflon Tape 🪣 Bucket
🛒 Shop Water Filters
⚠️ Safety First: Turn off cold water supply under sink. Open faucet to release pressure. Have a bucket ready — water will spill when disconnecting lines.
🔒
Step 1
Turn off cold water supply valve under sink. Clockwise to close. Open faucet to drain pressure.
🔧
Step 2
Disconnect cold water line from faucet using adjustable wrench. Have bucket ready — water will spill out.
📍
Step 3
Mount filter bracket inside cabinet — mark and drill pilot holes. Keep filter accessible for future cartridge changes.
🔗
Step 4
Connect filter inlet to cold water supply using included adapter. Wrap threads with Teflon tape — 3-4 wraps clockwise.
🚰
Step 5
Connect filter outlet to faucet cold water inlet. Hand-tighten first, then 1/2 turn with wrench. Don't overtighten.
🔩
Step 6
Install dedicated filter faucet through sink hole or countertop. May need to drill new hole — use hole saw appropriate for your sink material.
💧
Step 7
Turn water on and flush filter for 5 minutes before drinking. Removes carbon fines and activates the filter media.

Tip: Set a phone reminder to replace filter cartridges every 6 months — expired filters can harbor bacteria and stop removing contaminants.

Unclog a Sink Drain

beginner ⏱️ 15-30 minutes 💰 $0-15

Before calling a plumber for $150+, try these methods to clear a clogged sink yourself.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🪠 Plunger $10-15
  • 🧂 Baking Soda $2
  • 🍶 White Vinegar $3
  • 🪣 Bucket
⚠️ Safety First: Never use chemical drain cleaners after plunging — the chemicals can splash back and cause chemical burns.
  1. 🪣 Place a bucket under the P-trap to catch water before you start.
  2. 🪠 Use a cup plunger (not a toilet plunger) — seal the overflow drain with a wet rag first.
  3. 🧂 If plunging fails, pour 1/2 cup baking soda down the drain, followed by 1 cup vinegar.
  4. ⏳ Let the mixture fizz for 15 minutes, then flush with hot water for 2 minutes.
  5. 🔩 Remove and clean the P-trap (the U-shaped pipe under the sink) — unscrew by hand or with pliers.
  6. 🔄 Reassemble the trap hand-tight only — overtightening cracks plastic fittings.

💡 Pro Tip: For bathroom sinks, hair is usually the culprit. A $3 zip-it tool pulls out hair clogs without disassembling anything.

Fix a Running Toilet

beginner ⏱️ 20 minutes 💰 $10-25

A running toilet wastes 200+ gallons per day. Usually a 5-minute flapper or fill valve fix.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🛡️ Toilet Flapper $5-10
  • 🚰 Fill Valve $10-20
  • 🧽 Sponge
  • 🧻 Towel
⚠️ Safety First: The tank water is clean (same as your tap), but the bowl and rim jets are not. Keep tools separate from food prep areas.
  1. 🔍 Listen to identify the noise — hissing = fill valve, intermittent running = flapper.
  2. 🚫 Turn off water at the shutoff valve behind the toilet (turn clockwise).
  3. 🛡️ If flapper: drain tank, unhook old flapper from chain and overflow tube, install new one.
  4. 🔗 Adjust chain length — 1/2 inch of slack when closed. Too tight or too loose causes problems.
  5. 🚰 If fill valve: disconnect supply line, unscrew mounting nut, replace with new valve.
  6. 💧 Turn water back on, let tank fill, and verify the water level is 1 inch below overflow tube.

💡 Pro Tip: Put a few drops of food coloring in the tank — if color appears in the bowl without flushing, your flapper is leaking.

Replace a Kitchen Faucet

intermediate ⏱️ 60-90 minutes 💰 $100-300

A new faucet transforms your kitchen. With basic tools and patience, most homeowners can DIY this upgrade.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🚰 New Faucet $100-300
  • 🔧 Basin Wrench $15-25
  • 🪣 Plumber's Putty $3
  • 🎀 Teflon Tape $2
  • 🔧 Adjustable Wrench
⚠️ Safety First: Check your sink's hole configuration before buying — some faucets need 1 hole, others need 3. Adapters exist but add complexity.
  1. 🚫 Turn off hot and cold water shutoff valves under the sink (clockwise).
  2. 📸 Take photos of existing plumbing configuration before disconnecting anything.
  3. 🪣 Disconnect supply lines from old faucet and let remaining water drain into a bucket.
  4. 🔧 Remove mounting nuts with basin wrench (essential tool for tight spaces under sinks).
  5. 🪣 Apply plumber's putty under the new faucet base (or use included rubber gasket if provided).
  6. 🚰 Connect supply lines, turn water back on slowly, and check all connections for leaks.

💡 Pro Tip: Buy a faucet with flexible supply lines built in — they make installation much easier than rigid copper tubes.

Flush Your Water Heater

beginner ⏱️ 45-60 minutes 💰 $0

Sediment buildup reduces efficiency and lifespan. Annual flushing prevents premature failure and maintains hot water capacity.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🚿 Garden Hose
  • 🪛 Flathead Screwdriver
  • 🪣 Bucket
⚠️ Safety First: The water is HOT — let it cool for 2 hours before flushing, or wear heavy gloves and work carefully.
  1. 🚫 Turn off the gas valve (gas heater) or breaker (electric heater).
  2. 🚿 Connect garden hose to drain valve at bottom of tank, run to floor drain or outside.
  3. 🚰 Open a hot water faucet somewhere in the house to allow air into the tank.
  4. 🔩 Open the drain valve — water will flow slowly at first as sediment blocks the opening.
  5. 🌡️ Once draining, open the temperature-pressure relief (TPR) valve at top to speed flow.
  6. 🔄 When water runs clear, close drain valve, remove hose, refill tank, then restore power/gas.

💡 Pro Tip: If water is rusty or smells like sulfur, your anode rod needs replacement — a separate $20 repair that prevents tank corrosion.

Fix Low Water Pressure

intermediate ⏱️ 30-60 minutes 💰 $10-50

Low pressure affects one fixture or the whole house. Diagnose the cause and fix it without calling a plumber.

🛠️ Tools You'll Need

  • 🚰 Aerator $5-15
  • 🚿 Showerhead $15-40
  • 🍶 Vinegar
  • 🔧 Adjustable Wrench
⚠️ Safety First: Never set pressure above 80 PSI — it strains pipes, fixtures, and appliances. Most homes are happy at 50-60 PSI.
  1. 🔍 Check if the problem is one fixture or all fixtures — this tells you if it's localized or systemic.
  2. 🚰 For one faucet: unscrew the aerator (the screen at the tip), clean out debris, or replace it.
  3. 🚿 For one shower: remove showerhead and soak in vinegar overnight to dissolve mineral deposits.
  4. 🏠 For whole house: check the main shutoff valve — it may be partially closed from recent work.
  5. 🔧 Check the pressure regulator (usually near main shutoff) — adjust screw clockwise to increase pressure.
  6. 📏 If pressure is still low, test at an outdoor spigot — if that's low too, call the water company.

💡 Pro Tip: Low hot water pressure only? Check if your water heater shutoff valve is partially closed — it's often forgotten after maintenance.
